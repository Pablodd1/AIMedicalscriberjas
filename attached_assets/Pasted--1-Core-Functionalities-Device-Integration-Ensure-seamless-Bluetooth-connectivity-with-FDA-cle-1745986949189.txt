Example: Patient Data Visualization Report
1. Patient Summary Dashboard
•	Overview:
o	Displays key metrics at a glance, such as average BP and glucose levels over the past week or month.
o	Highlights any readings outside the normal range with color-coded indicators (e.g., red for high, blue for low).
•	Features:
o	Trend Graphs:
	Line charts showing daily BP and glucose readings over time.
	Ability to overlay medication intake times to correlate with readings.
o	Alerts:
	Notifications for readings that exceed predefined thresholds.
	Summary of alerts sent and actions taken.
2. Detailed Reports
•	Blood Pressure Report:
o	Provides a weekly or monthly breakdown of systolic and diastolic readings.
o	Includes average, highest, and lowest readings.
o	Visual representation using bar charts or line graphs.
•	Glucose Monitoring Report:
o	Displays fasting, pre-meal, and post-meal glucose levels.
o	Highlights patterns or trends, such as consistent post-meal spikes.
o	Incorporates pie charts to show percentage of readings within target range.Wikipedia+1Wikipedia+1
3. Interactive Elements
•	Date Range Selector:
o	Allows users to filter data by specific dates or periods.
•	Data Export:
o	Option to download reports in PDF or CSV format for sharing with healthcare providers.
•	Notes Section:
o	Enables patients to add comments or observations related to specific readings.
